Starting your first corporate job is exciting and overwhelming in equal measure. The first 90 days are your golden window — this is when impressions are formed, relationships are built, and your professional reputation begins to take shape.

## Why the First 90 Days Matter

Research consistently shows that employees who make a strong start in their first three months are significantly more likely to succeed long-term. During this period:

- **Leaders form opinions** about your potential
- **Peers decide** whether you are a team player
- **You establish habits** that will define your work style

## The 30-60-90 Framework

### Days 1-30: Listen and Learn

Your first month should be about absorption:

- **Ask questions** — intelligent questions show engagement, not weakness
- **Understand the culture** — observe how people communicate, what is valued, and how decisions are made
- **Map the stakeholders** — identify who influences decisions and build those relationships early
- **Document everything** — keep notes on processes, expectations, and key contacts

### Days 31-60: Contribute and Connect

Month two is about demonstrating value:

- **Take ownership** of small tasks and deliver them excellently
- **Volunteer for projects** that give you visibility across teams
- **Seek feedback** proactively — do not wait for formal reviews
- **Build your internal network** — have coffee chats with colleagues from different departments

### Days 61-90: Lead and Expand

By month three, you should be hitting your stride:

- **Propose improvements** you have identified during your observation phase
- **Mentor newer team members** if applicable
- **Set goals** with your manager for the next quarter
- **Make your value visible** — share wins in team meetings and update stakeholders on progress

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Staying invisible** — do not just put your head down and work. Visibility matters.
2. **Not asking for help** — struggling silently wastes time and creates anxiety
3. **Ignoring office culture** — technical skills get you hired, cultural fit keeps you there
4. **Comparing to college** — the workplace has different rules. Adapt quickly.
